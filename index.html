Oddly enough this was part of a tight childhood soundtrack to accompany RuneScape (Classic). Paired in the context of the early James Blake singles & EPs the underlying soundscape of UK postdubstep persists in driving forward a bass heavy sound and spacially vibrant vocal samples. I would somewhat attribute this early singles of Blake to be almost reliant on the 'Bass Boost' focused market trend of cheaper consumer-grade ear/headphones. I would speculate 95% of my James Blake consumpsion has been through the likes of SkullCandyz and related Target/Walmart plucked sub-$20 earbuds. I would also speculate 95% of any early hearing loss to said earbuds. -A



I have an issue of arbitrarily drawing a line in an artist's discography and categorizing their catalog as 'New' and 'Old'. Break It Yourself sits just south of that line, capping off what I will trivially label as Andrew Bird's 'Old' catalog. Bird's use of layering violin into the mix continues in weaving a dreamy and nostalgic atmosphere that envelops the listener. -A
